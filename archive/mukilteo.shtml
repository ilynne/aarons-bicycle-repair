Another wet, cold, happy group.

These trails are really fun and fairly close to Seattle. There is a lot of 'North Shore' style ramps and sky bridges. Lots of unfinished pieces. Some of the stuff is just plain nuts. Most of it is well constructed and ridable, but not by us! Someone rides it......It is just that a 6 foot drop-off with only a 20 foot run out takes a lot of practice (crashing) to be able to do.

The temperature was in the high 30s but since the trail starts off on a hill, we were plenty warm. It started to snow at the top. There are lots of trails and plenty of obstacles to ride on. Most have ride-arounds too. The ride down was really fun and fast. We took the road because Anne's freewheel decided to freewheel both ways! At least it happened at the top of the hill.

To get there:
Take I-5 north to the Mukilteo Speedway. Follow it almost to the ferry. Turn Right on 5th Avenue (turns into Mulkilteo BLVD). Park down on the right just before the bridge over the RR tracks. The trails are off to your right across a small field.
